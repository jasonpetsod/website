--- title: Braised Duck with Green Beans tags: - recipes active_time: 20 minutes total_time: 2 hours serves: 4 adapted_from: Bitten Blog --- ### Ingredients * 4 duck legs or 1 duck, cut into quarters * salt and pepper * 1 large onion, sliced * 1 tbsp ginger, minced * 1 tbsp garlic, minced * 2 [Thai peppers](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thai_pepper), minced * 1 1/2 lb green beans, trimmed * 1 tbsp sugar, or to taste * 2 tbsp nam pla * 2 tbsp lime juice, or to taste * coarsely chopped fresh cilantro leaves for garnish, optional ### Procedure 1. Season duck with salt and pepper. Put it in a skillet that will fit it comfortably, turn heat to medium, and cover. 1. Once you hear sizzling, duck should be simmering in its own fat and exuding liquid. Adjust heat to create a steady simmer. 1. Once bottom browns, turn. Eventually liquid will evaporate and duck will cook in fat only. At this point, turn heat to low and continue to cook duck, turning once in a while, until it becomes tender, about an hour. 1. Transfer duck to a plate. Pour off all but a couple of tablespoons of fat. 1. Turn heat to medium high, and add onion; cook, stirring occasionally, until it softens, about 5 minutes. 1. Add ginger, garlic, and peppers and cook, stirring, for 30 seconds. 1. Add beans and sugar and turn heat to high; cook, stirring occasionally, until beans begin to brown, about 5 minutes. 4. Add 2 tablespoons water and nam pla. Put duck on top of bean mixture and bring to a simmer. Cover and cook until both beans and duck are very tender, 15 to 30 more minutes, adding a little more water if necessary to keep mixture moist. (You can prepare dish in advance up to this point; cover and set aside until ready to eat, then reheat.) 1. Uncover and stir in lime juice; taste and adjust seasoning, then sprinkle with cilantro and serve.
