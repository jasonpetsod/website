--- title: Chicken Curry tags: - recipes active_time: 30 minutes total_time: 1 hour, 30 minutes serves: 4 to 6 adapted_from: Mark Bittman, How to Cook Everything --- ### Ingredients * 2 tbsp canola oil * 2 lb chicken thighs, cut into vaguely bite-sized pieces * salt and pepper * 1 large onion; half chopped, half thinly sliced * 3 cloves of garlic, minced * 1 tbsp ginger, peeled and minced * 1 1/2 cup coconut milk (can substitute yogurt for some or all) * 1/2 cup chicken stock * cilantro leaves, minced #### Spices * 1/2 tsp black peppercorns * 1/8 tsp nutmeg * 3 cloves * 1/3 tsp cardamom seeds * 1-inch cinnamon stick (substitution: 1 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon) * 4 tsp cumin seeds * 5 tsp coriander seeds * 1 tsp ground ginger * 2 tsp ground tumeric * 1/2 tsp ground cayenne (less if you're spice-averse) ### Procedure 1. Toast and grind first seven spices. Mix in ginger, tumeric, and cayenne. Set aside. (If whole spices aren't available, substitute ground versions and don't toast. Also, a zip-loc bag and a rolling pin can be used as a grinder in a pinch.) 1. Heat oil over medium-high heat in large, deep skillet that can later be covered. 1. Brown chicken chunks in batches, seasoning with salt and pepper as they cook, about 8 minutes per batch. 1. Remove chicken and discard all but 2 tbsp of fat. 1. Cook sliced onions over medium heat until golden brown, about 6 minutes. 1. Add chopped onion, garlic, ginger, and spices. Stir and cook over medium heat for 3 to 4 minutes until chopped onion softens. 1. Return chicken to pot. Add 1 cup of coconut milk and all of the stock, scraping off the browned bits on the bottom of the pan. The mix might look a little dry, but that should be fine; we're adding more coconut milk at the end. Bring to a boil. 1. Cover and turn heat to low. Simmer until chicken is tender and cooked through, at least 1 hour. 1. Remove the cover. If the mixture is soupy, turn heat to high and reduce it a bit. Taste and adjust seasoning. 1. Stir in remaining coconut milk. Serve with steamed jasmine or basmati rice, garnished with cilantro.
